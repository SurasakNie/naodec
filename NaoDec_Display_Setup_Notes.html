2. Space & Structure

  • Total allocated floor area: 4 m × 5.5 m = 22 m².
  • Structure footprint: 3 m × 3 m = 9 m², sitting inside the allocated area.
  • Structure height: 2.5 m (W × L × H = 3 m × 3 m × 2.5 m). It fits under the store’s minimum 2.75 m ceiling clearance with ~0.25 m margin; keep that margin clear of the platform/riser.
  • The operator station (table, controller unit, Mac mini, monitor) sits outside the 3×3 m structure zone, in the remaining ~13 m² of the allocated area.
The structure height sits exactly at the template’s “>2.5 m mandatory stability-certification” threshold, not strictly over it. Certification is still recommended because the structure is occupied and carries a ~345 kg load.

3. Power Requirement — Full Derivation

The rule: Provisioning target = 1.25× the current setup’s continuous operating draw, main input 220 V single-phase, 10 hrs/day operation (so no backup power requirement).

Notes: The six speakers and four chair transducers are passive, driven by the GAB8 (item F), not separate loads. The vertex-strip 12 VDC power injection redistributes existing current, adding no new load. The full-white LED figure (15 mA/px) is already the worst case.

3.2 The 1.25× calculation

Current continuous draw:      792 W
1.25× design target:          792 × 1.25 = 990 W  ≈  1.0 kW
Worst-case transient:         1,133 W (all rated max) + 240 W (both actuators) = 1,373 W ≈ 1.37 kW

Converted to current @ 220 V single-phase (power factor ≈ 0.9 assumed):

ConditionPowerCurrent
Continuous operating792 W~4.0 A
1.25× target (the deliverable)990 W~5.0 A
Worst-case transient1,373 W~6.9 A

Daily energy: 792 W × 10 h ≈ ~7.9 kWh/day (~238 kWh/month).

3.3 Circuit recommendation

Provide one dedicated 16 A, 220 V single-phase circuit (TIS 166-2549 socket), running at only ~28% load at the 1.25× target. It comfortably absorbs the 1.37 kW transient plus actuator/SMPS inrush. A 20 A circuit also works if the store prefers more margin. Add surge protection, an accessible kill switch, and a fused sub-distribution at the operator station feeding the control box (which keeps its own 10 A MCB + inrush limiter), AV/host station, GAB8 24 V PSU, subwoofer, and both actuators.

3.4 What we couldn’t get better numbers for

Neither the GAB8 amp nor the Edifier T5 publish typical AC consumption. Manufacturers specify RMS output and recommended PSU rating; actual draw is program-dependent. The GAB8 manual recommends the Mean Well LRS-350-24/N2 — the PSU already chosen — confirming it is correctly matched. The ~120 W (GAB8) and ~70 W (T5) figures are engineering estimates, cited to manufacturer sources.

Key finding: Weight is not the constraint; size is. The chair platform’s 1.91 m × 1.82 m footprint (diagonal ≈ 2.64 m) is the largest component and may not fit an elevator or standard doorway flat. Confirm actual elevator cab/door dimensions before committing to elevator delivery; freight lift, wide-corridor routing, or sectional shipping may be needed.

5.2 Logistics questions still open with the store

  • Move-in/setup time allowance — is overnight setup possible?
  • Drop-off/loading gate location — ground level or an upper-floor gate, and distance to the display area?
  • Floor protection — does the plaza supply mats/runners for the move-in path, or do we bring our own?
  • Trolley/hand-truck — available to borrow? Size and maximum load rating?
  • Elevator — cab interior dimensions, door clear-opening width, and weight capacity (check against the 1.91 × 1.82 m chair platform specifically).

# Paint Comparison for Wood Work: Maxzo Rust Tech 2IN1 #8300 vs. JBP Smart Glaze (Ultra Gloss Enamel & 2IN1)

Reference notes comparing three commercially available oil/alkyd-type coatings for painting wood
(and mixed wood + metal builds), with cautions specific to wood application.

## Products covered

1. **Maxzo Rust Tech 2IN1 #8300** — matte black, combines anti-rust primer + topcoat in one product.
   Primary use case is metal (steel, galvanized steel, zinc-plated steel, aluminum, stainless steel);
   the label also lists plywood, wood siding board, and all types of artificial/composite wood as
   compatible surfaces — solid/hardwood is not listed.
2. **JBP Smart Glaze Ultra Gloss Enamel** — oil-based alkyd resin gloss enamel, topcoat only (no
   built-in primer). Labeled for both wood and metal (doors, windows, furniture, machinery).
3. **JBP Smart Glaze 2IN1** — acrylic-resin based, combines anti-rust primer + topcoat in one
   product, explicitly labeled for metal, non-metal, and wood surfaces, interior and exterior.

## Cautions when painting wood

- **Maxzo Rust Tech** is formulated first for rust prevention on metal. Its wood compatibility on
  the label is limited to plywood/siding/composite wood — not solid wood. The rigid rust-inhibitive
  film is not designed to flex with wood's moisture-driven expansion/contraction, so it may be more
  prone to cracking along the grain on solid wood over time. Test on a small area first.
- **JBP Smart Glaze Ultra Gloss Enamel** is a topcoat only — bare wood should get a wood primer/
  sealer coat first, otherwise the oil-based enamel absorbs unevenly into porous wood (knots, end
  grain), producing a patchy sheen and wasting material.
- **Drying times matter for scheduling.** JBP Ultra Gloss: touch-dry in 30 min–4 hr, recoat window
  4–8 hr, full/hard cure 48 hr+. Don't load or stack painted pieces before full cure, and protect
  the work from dust, insects, and rain throughout the cure period. Recoating outside the 4–8 hr
  window (i.e., after the film has hardened too much) needs a light sanding pass first for proper
  intercoat adhesion; recoating too early (before the lower coat has released enough solvent) risks
  wrinkling/lifting.
- **Use only the manufacturer-specified thinner** — Maxzo requires AAA thinner and explicitly warns
  against turpentine; using the wrong solvent affects film adhesion and finish.
- **Gloss vs. matte surface prep**: gloss finishes (both JBP products) show surface defects clearly,
  so sand and fill wood before coating. Matte (Maxzo) is more forgiving of surface texture.
- **Don't casually layer products from different lines** (e.g., a Maxzo matte base under a JBP gloss
  topcoat) without a test patch — different alkyd/acrylic formulations and solvent strengths can
  cause poor intercoat adhesion or wrinkling if recoat timing isn't respected.
- **Solvent-based safety**: all three products are flammable while wet, need good ventilation, and
  require PPE (gloves, respirator) during application. Oil-soaked rags carry a spontaneous
  combustion risk — lay them flat to dry outdoors or submerge in water before disposal; never bunch
  them into a trash bin.
- **Label mix-up risk**: JBP sells both "Smart Glaze Ultra Gloss Enamel" (topcoat only) and "Smart
  Glaze 2IN1" (primer + topcoat) under similar names — confirm "2IN1" is on the label if a
  self-priming product is what's needed.
- Neither Maxzo nor JBP 2IN1 states anti-fungal/anti-algae or lead/mercury-free properties on their
  listings (unlike JBP Ultra Gloss, which states both) — verify on the physical can label if this
  matters for the application (e.g., children's furniture).

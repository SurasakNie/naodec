Series Coil / Electromagnet Subsystem

NaoDec · Rev 1.0 · 60-coil series string on 12 V DC

Overview

Sixty hand-wound copper coils (≈7 turns of ~65 cm of 1 mm copper each, ~3 cm diameter, air core) wired end-to-end in a single series loop and driven from a 12 V DC supply. Coils are grouped into 20 sets of 3, joined by 2-core 24 AWG flat cable and 2-pin JST connectors, with a 6 m return run to the PSU. Intended purpose: electromagnetic field from the coils.

⚠ The key reality — an inductor does nothing on steady-state DC.

Inductor impedance is Z = jωL; at DC, ω = 0, so each coil is simply a piece of wire. This circuit is therefore a ~4 Ω resistive near-short with no current-limiting element. Each individual coil is only ~0.014 Ω — the steady current is set entirely by the total wire resistance and whatever the PSU will deliver.

⚠ The protective paradox — do not "fix" the inefficiency.

The cable resistance (~3.3 Ω of the ~4 Ω) is the only thing keeping the current down to a non-destructive ~3 A, because each coil is a near short. Shortening or thickening the cable to recover the wasted voltage will let the current run away (e.g. ~0.5 Ω cable → ~9 A), which will overheat the 24 AWG wire and the JST connectors. If you want lower cable loss, add a real current limiter — never just lower the resistance.

Will the cable burn?

With a current-limited 12 V supply (≤3 A) plus an inline fuse: no. The 24 AWG runs warm (~0.7 W/m; open-air ampacity ~3.5 A), the coils stay cool, and the JST connectors are the weak link (see below). Keep the 6 m run and any slack uncoiled so heat can escape.

Without current limiting / a fuse on a large supply or battery — or if the cable resistance is reduced — yes, there is an overheat risk. The connectors cook before the wire does: JST PH is rated 2 A (over), XH / SM ~3 A (at the limit).

Recommended supply

  • Best: a 12 V bench/lab supply with adjustable constant-current (CC) limit, set to ~3 A. It physically cannot exceed the set current, gives a current readout, and lets you experiment safely (e.g. when adding cores).
  • If fixed brick: 12 V, 3–5 A, with a 3–4 A inline fuse at V+.
  • Avoid: putting this near-short on a large unprotected supply (e.g. the installation's 12 V 50 A edge rail) or a battery without a fuse.

Safety checklist

  • Fuse it (3–4 A inline at V+) — non-negotiable; the load is a near-short.
  • Current-limit it — prefer a CC bench supply set to ~3 A.
  • Don't coil the 6 m run or slack cable — coiled cable can't shed heat.
  • Power down before unplugging any JST — breaking an inductive loop under load arcs and pits contacts.
  • Don't reduce cable resistance without a current limiter — current will run away.
  • Keep this V+ rail isolated from the 5 V / 12 V 5 A / 12 V 50 A LED rails, per the NaoDec power-rail rule.
